ABOUT

The Chinese Agricultural Scientist Database (CASDB) is a relational database that aims to compile biographical information for every agricultural scientists from China during the first half of the twentieth century. This includes basic biographical data in addition to information on education, employment, and scientific publications. The database allows researchers to discover information about individual scientists or to conduct larger-scale statistical, spatial, or social network analyses.

SOURCES

Sources for the first phase of the project come from personnel records (see figure 1) from the Archives of the Institute of Modern History at Academia Sinica, Taipei, Taiwan. Personnel records were collected for scientists affiliated with central and local agricultural research institutions in the 1940s. To date, we have collected personnel files for around five thousand scientists working at over 50 different institutions throughout China. We are still in the process of transcribing the records and will make them available when completed.

Figure 1: Personnel Record for Sun Enlin

After transcribing the personnel records, we converted the files into an SQL relational database containing seven tables with their respective variables as found in Figure 2. Different colored boxes represent how the tables are connected to one another. The Education, Employment, Publications, and Social tables are connected to the Scientist table through the SCIENTIST_PK/FK variable (Red). The Education and Employment tables are linked to the Institution table through the INST_PK/FK variable (Blue). And the Scientist and Institution tables are linked to the Geography table through the GEO_PK/FK variable (Green). The Publications table was supplemented with metadata from the Shanghai Library's 全国报刊索引 and the 大成老旧刊全文数据库.
