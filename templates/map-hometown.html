Below are several maps showing the hometowns for over 400 scientists employed by the National Agricultural Research Bureau 中央農業實驗所 (NARB) in the mid to late 1940s. According to the data, 24.7% of scientists (108) were from Jiangsu and 15.8% (69) were from Zhejiang. This is relatively consistent with the 1935 membership records for the Agricultural Association of China 中華農學會 (AAC) containing records for 2,330 agronomists (this data shows that nearly 30 percent of scientists were from Jiangsu and 13 percent from Zhejiang).

Further exploration of the data highlights a general geographic shift overtime, which accounts for the lower percentage of scientists born in Jiangsu in the NARB data compared to the AAC data. The maps below plots the hometown for scientists born in different decades. It shows that overtime, a growing number of scientists at NARB were born in other parts of the country, especially Western China. The relocation of NARB to Sichuan with the outbreak of the Sino-Japanese War in 1937 was a major contributing factor in the incorporation of local talent into the agricultural research at NARB.
